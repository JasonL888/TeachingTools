# Issue
- On MacOS, if using Microsoft Teams to share screen, some how the iOS screen mirroring does NOT work
    - get a blank screen

# Workaround
## Method 1: The QuickTime Wired Workaround (Most Reliable)
The most bulletproof way to share your iOS screen on Teams without getting blocked by DRM is to pass the video feed through QuickTime Player using a physical cable. Teams recognizes this as a standard media playback window rather than a protected system stream.

1. Connect your iPhone to your Mac:
> Requires a USB-C or Lightning cable.
Plug your iPhone directly into your Mac. If prompted on your iPhone, unlock it and select Trust This Computer.

2. Open QuickTime Player:
> Built-in Mac application
Launch QuickTime Player on your Mac. Do not go to open a file; instead, look at the top menu bar.

3. Start a New Movie Recording:
> Do not click record
Go to File > New Movie Recording. A camera window will pop up (it will likely default to your Mac's webcam).

4. Switch the input to your iPhone:
> Crucial step.
Click the tiny downward arrow (∨) right next to the red Record button in the QuickTime window. 
Under `Screen`, select your iPhone. 
Your phone's screen will instantly appear on your Mac.

5. Share the QuickTime Window in Teams:
> In your Teams meeting
In Microsoft Teams, click Share, choose Window (instead of your entire desktop), and select the QuickTime Player window.
